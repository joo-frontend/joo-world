02

What is TIFA?

TIFA (Tizen Identifier For Advertising) is a unique,
randomized, and user-resettable identifier generated by Samsung to increase your privacy as a Smart TV user.
TIFA identifies your Smart TV, and is not connected to any Personally Identifiable Information (PII).

03

How is it used?

TIFA is used by Samsung, OTT TV Apps and Content Partners, and other advertising partners (hereafter “Partners”) to deliver personalized advertisements on Samsung Smart TV.

04

How do Partners access TIFA?

OTT TV Apps and Content Partners access and collect TIFA directly from your Smart TV using their own Apps, and send it to advertising partners. Samsung restricts partners from using TIFA for anything other than advertising, and imposes an obligation to comply with all applicable privacy and data protection laws and regulations. Samsung regularly audits for compliance with terms of use on a best effort basis.

05

Why was TIFA introduced?

With the growth of advertising in streaming content and services on Smart TV, TIFA was introduced to increase your privacy as a Smart TV user. For increased security –

  • TIFA is hashed PSID*.
  • You can reset it anytime by resetting the PSID, or by performing a factory reset
    of the Samsung Smart TV, using the TV's “Settings” menu.
  • You can opt-out of personalized ads and tracking.
  • TIFA is for advertising purposes only, with no connection to any PII
    (Personally Identifiable Information).

* PSID is a former Samsung Smart TV identifier for advertisement which Samsung intends to use internally followed by the introduction of TIFA. Any reference to PSID on 2019, 2018, 2017, or 2016 TV’s “Settings” menu running the latest Tizen software version, should be read as TIFA.
