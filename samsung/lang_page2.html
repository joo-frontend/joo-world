01

What is interest-based advertisement?

What is interest-based
advertisement?

Interest-based advertisement allows Samsung to deliver relevant ads based on viewing behavior and device usage which reflects your household interest. Interest-based advertisement will be provided only if we have obtained your consent for interest-based advertisement and viewing information services, respectively. If you consent only for interest-based advertisement and not for viewing information services, Samsung delivers ads based only on device usage.

03

How Other Household Members May See Ads

How Other Household Members
May See Ads

Most TVs are shared devices in a household, with multiple members watching content.
Different members in the household have different tastes. Some people love live sports, while others enjoy action movies.
Some prefer binge-watching sci-fi series on-demand, while others prefer one-off comedy shows and documentaries. There might be a gamer in the house or even a family tuned-in to cartoons.

TV data we use for interest-based advertisement is based on the shared viewing experiences of the entire household. As a result, you might see ads based on another household member’s interests.
This is because we do not identify or use data from any individual person using the TV.

04

How does interest-based advertising work

How does interest-based
advertising work

If you have opted-in to interest-based advertising, our aim is to show you relevant ads so you may discover new content or learn more about a product. We use viewing behavior and device usage data that reflects your household interests, combined with data from trusted third-party providers. For advertising across connected devices, Samsung uses data from third-party websites. All of this is done at the household level.

Samsung will use data associated with your smart TV’s unique Advertising ID (TIFA) and its Internet Protocol (IP) address to enable interest-based advertising on computers, smartphones, or tablets.

For example, if you have tuned into superhero movies on your smart TV, an advertiser for a new superhero movie or TV series might target advertising to you when you are on a computer, smartphone, or tablet that has been connected to the same IP address.

As another example, if you have shopped for football equipment on your laptop, an advertiser might target advertising to you on your smart TV for tickets to football games or other sports equipment.

Most of the data we use for interest-based advertising comes from the Samsung TV itself. This data includes :

  • TV Advertising IDs (called a PSID and TIFA)
  • IP Address (e.g. the internet protocol address your TV uses to connect to the Internet)
  • TV App Usage (e.g. frequently launched on-demand subscription apps)
  • TV Connected Devices
    (e.g. games console, set-top box, streaming device)
  • TV Viewing Information, with your separate consent
    (e.g. content viewed - live sports, sci-fi movies, stand-up comedy)
We use data from trusted third-party companies to better understand your household’s interests. These third-party companies have already collected personal information from you through other sources and means. All third-party companies are required to use privacy-compliant means.

07

What do advertisers know about you?

Interest-based advertisements are only delivered to large groups of devices. Advertisers only know they are reaching a large group of people who might be interested in what they are offering.

Once ads are delivered, we share insights reports with advertisers, so they can understand how their ads are performing. Samsung does not supply to advertisers any personally identifiable information about you, such as your name, address, or email.
